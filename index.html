About

I am an Associate Professor in the Department of Cognitive Science at the University of Somewhere, where I lead the Computational Mind Lab. My work sits at the intersection of cognitive psychology, neuroscience, and machine learning.

Before joining the faculty at Somewhere, I completed my PhD at MIT and was a postdoctoral fellow at Stanford's Human-Centered AI Institute. I have been awarded the NSF CAREER Award and the Young Investigator Prize from the Cognitive Science Society.

Outside of research, I am deeply committed to open science, reproducibility, and making academic knowledge accessible to broader audiences.

Research

01

Computational Cognition

Developing Bayesian models of human reasoning and decision-making under uncertainty, with applications to clinical and educational settings.

02

Language & Memory

Investigating how language shapes and retrieves episodic memories, combining behavioral experiments with neuroimaging methods.

03

Human-AI Collaboration

Studying how humans and AI systems can effectively collaborate on complex cognitive tasks, with a focus on trust and error recovery.

Teaching
